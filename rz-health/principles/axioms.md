# rz-health Axioms

## 1. Health is the Platform

Every other skill in the RZ Stack runs on your body and mind. Degraded health degrades everything. Protect this foundation above all else.

## 2. Consistency Over Intensity

A moderate workout done 5 days a week beats an extreme session once a week. Build habits that are sustainable for decades, not programs that burn you out in months.

## 3. Evidence-Based Protocols

Follow practices supported by peer-reviewed research, not influencer trends. When in doubt, consult qualified professionals (doctors, registered dietitians, certified trainers).

## 4. Progressive Overload

Improvement requires gradually increasing demands — more weight, more reps, more distance, better quality. Staying comfortable means stagnating.

## 5. Recovery is Training

Muscles grow during rest, not during the workout. Sleep, rest days, and deload periods are not optional — they are part of the program.

## 6. Nutrition is the Foundation

You cannot out-train a bad diet. Prioritize whole foods, adequate protein, and sufficient calories for your goals. Supplements supplement — they don't replace.

## 7. Sleep is Non-Negotiable

7-9 hours of quality sleep. Consistent schedule. Dark, cool room. No screens before bed. Sleep deprivation destroys cognitive performance, recovery, and mood — all of which cascade into every other skill.

## 8. Measure What Matters

Track key metrics (body weight, training volume, sleep duration, energy levels) to detect trends. But don't let tracking become obsessive — the goal is information, not anxiety.

## 9. Mental Health is Health

Meditation, journaling, therapy, social connection — these are not luxuries. Chronic stress and isolation undermine physical health directly. Invest in mental wellness proactively.

## 10. Injury Prevention Over Performance

No single workout is worth an injury that sidelines you for months. Warm up, use proper form, listen to pain signals, and scale back when something feels wrong.
