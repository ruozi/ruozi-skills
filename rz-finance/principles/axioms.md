# rz-finance Axioms

## 1. Pay Yourself First

Savings and investment allocations happen before discretionary spending. Automate this. Willpower is unreliable; systems are not.

## 2. Evidence Over Narrative

Every investment thesis requires data. Market narratives, influencer hype, and gut feelings are not investment strategies. Apply the rz-data-analysis pipeline to financial decisions.

## 3. Compound Interest is the Engine

Time is the most powerful variable. Start early, stay consistent, and let compounding do the heavy lifting. Avoid interrupting compound growth for short-term gains.

## 4. Fees Are the Silent Killer

Minimize expense ratios, transaction costs, and tax drag. A 1% fee difference compounds to massive wealth destruction over decades.

## 5. Diversify Structurally

Diversify across asset classes, geographies, sectors, and time (dollar-cost averaging). Concentration builds wealth; diversification preserves it.

## 6. Risk is What You Don't See

The biggest risks are the ones you haven't considered. Maintain an emergency fund (6+ months). Insure against catastrophic loss. Size positions so no single failure is fatal.

## 7. Know Your Edge (Or Admit You Don't Have One)

If you can't articulate why you have an edge in a specific investment, use index funds. Most active strategies underperform passive ones after fees.

## 8. Separate Emotion from Decision

Fear and greed are the enemies of good financial decisions. Create rules in advance (rebalancing triggers, stop-losses, contribution schedules) and follow them mechanically.

## 9. Track Everything

What gets measured gets managed. Track net worth monthly, review portfolio quarterly, audit strategy annually. Use the rz-data-analysis six-step pipeline for reviews.

## 10. Financial Independence is Freedom

The goal of finance management is not maximum wealth — it's optionality. Financial independence means your time is yours. Every financial decision should be evaluated against this north star.
