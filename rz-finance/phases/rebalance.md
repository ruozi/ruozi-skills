# REBALANCE Phase

> Goal: Adjust portfolio and strategy based on data and life changes. This is where discipline meets adaptability. Sell decisions are rare and must survive rigorous scrutiny.

#### Entry Condition

Triggered by:
- Portfolio drift exceeds 5% from target allocation
- Business fundamentals have changed for a holding
- Major life event (job change, marriage, child, relocation)
- Annual review cycle
- Market dislocation creating exceptional buying opportunities
- Tax-loss harvesting opportunity

#### Core Workflow

1. **Diagnose the trigger** — Why is rebalancing needed?
   - Drift from target? → Mechanical rebalance
   - Fundamental change in a holding? → Re-evaluate through investing checklist
   - Life change? → Reassess risk profile, possibly redesign plan
   - Market panic? → Opportunity assessment, not panic response

2. **For sell decisions — apply strict criteria:**

   Before selling any position, it must fail at least one of these tests:

   | Sell Criterion | Question to Answer |
   |---------------|-------------------|
   | Permanent deterioration | Has the moat broken (not just temporary headwind)? |
   | Thesis invalidated | Were you wrong about the business? Is it outside your circle after all? |
   | Superior opportunity | Is the new opportunity clearly better AND does it require this capital? |
   | Extreme overvaluation | Is the price > 2x your intrinsic value estimate? |

   **The Buffett test:** "Would I buy this today at today's price?"
   - If yes → hold, regardless of what the price has done since you bought
   - If no → ask why. If the business changed, consider selling. If only the price changed, wait

3. **For buy decisions during rebalancing:**
   - Full Long-Term Investing Checklist applies (no shortcuts because "it's just a rebalance")
   - Market panics are opportunities — "Be greedy when others are fearful"
   - Only deploy cash reserves if the opportunity passes all checklist steps

4. **Tax-loss harvesting** (if applicable):
   - Sell losing positions to realize tax losses
   - Reinvest in similar (not identical) assets to maintain exposure
   - Be aware of wash sale rules
   - Log all harvesting actions with rationale

5. **Update documentation:**
   - Updated allocation targets (if risk profile changed)
   - Investment journal entries for all buy/sell decisions
   - Reason for every change (future you will want to know why)

#### Rules

- **One change at a time** — Don't overhaul the entire portfolio in one session
- **48-hour rule for emotional decisions** — If the urge to sell comes from fear (market crash) or greed (taking profits), wait 48 hours and re-evaluate
- **Never sell into panic** — Forced selling during downturns is how permanent capital loss happens
- **Document before acting** — Write down the reason for the change before executing it. If you can't articulate it clearly, don't act

#### Interaction Rules

- **Challenge every sell** — "Why are you selling? Has the business changed, or has the price changed? These are very different things."
- **Challenge FOMO buys** — "Is this inside your circle of competence? Does it pass the full checklist? Or are you buying because it went up?"
- **Enforce the 48-hour rule** — "You're reacting to a price movement. Wait 48 hours, then re-evaluate based on business fundamentals."

#### Exit Condition

- Portfolio within target allocation range
- All changes documented with clear rationale
- Investment journal updated for every buy/sell
- Tax-loss harvesting logged (if executed)
- Lessons captured for future monitoring cycles
- If life change triggered rebalance: updated plan reflects new reality
