# PLAN Phase

> Goal: Define financial goals and the strategy to achieve them. Strategy before execution — do the right thing, then do things right.

#### Entry Condition

ASSESS complete, goals need setting or updating.

#### Core Workflow

1. **Set goals** — Specific, measurable, time-bound:
   - Short-term (< 1 year): emergency fund, debt paydown, specific purchases
   - Medium-term (1-5 years): down payment, career transition fund, education
   - Long-term (5+ years): financial independence, retirement, legacy
   - Calculate FI number: annual expenses × 25 (4% rule baseline)

2. **Determine investment path** — Based on ASSESS results:
   - **Path A (Active Value Investing)**: You have genuine edge, time, and temperament for deep analysis. Proceed with concentrated portfolio of 5-10 high-conviction positions
   - **Path B (Passive Indexing)**: You don't have edge, time, or temperament. Low-cost index funds with dollar-cost averaging
   - **Be honest about which path you belong on.** Most people belong on Path B, and there is no shame in it — "A low-cost index fund is the most sensible equity investment for the great majority of investors" (Buffett)

3. **Asset allocation** — Target allocation by asset class:
   - Based on risk profile and time horizon from ASSESS
   - For Path A: equity allocation to individual stocks (within circle of competence), cash reserve for opportunities (20-30%)
   - For Path B: age-appropriate stock/bond split, total market index funds
   - Include geographic diversification if applicable

4. **Investment thesis development** (Path A only) — For each current or prospective holding:
   - Run the full Long-Term Investing Checklist (see `methods/long-term-investing.md`)
   - Document thesis in investment journal
   - Define review triggers (what would cause you to sell?)

5. **Tax strategy** — Maximize tax-advantaged accounts:
   - Contribute to tax-deferred and tax-free accounts first
   - Plan for tax-loss harvesting opportunities
   - Consider asset location (which investments in which account types)

6. **Debt strategy** — If applicable:
   - Compare debt interest rates to expected investment returns
   - High-interest debt (> 6-7%): pay down aggressively before investing
   - Low-interest debt (< 4%): invest excess after minimum payments

7. **Stop Doing List** — Define explicitly:
   - What investments are outside your circle of competence?
   - What behaviors will you avoid? (leverage, short selling, market timing, FOMO buying)
   - What spending patterns will you eliminate?

#### Interaction Rules

- **Challenge vague goals** — "Save more money" is not a goal. "Save $2,000/month to reach $500K invested by 2030" is
- **Test Path A conviction** — If the user claims Path A, quiz them on their top holding's business model, moat, and competitive dynamics. If they can't pass, recommend Path B
- **Enforce the Stop Doing List** — Require explicit commitments about what NOT to do

#### Exit Condition

- Goals are specific and time-bound (not "save more")
- Investment path chosen (A or B) with honest self-assessment
- Asset allocation matches risk profile from ASSESS phase
- For Path A: investment theses documented for all positions
- Stop Doing List defined and committed to
- Contribution plan is realistic given current cash flow
