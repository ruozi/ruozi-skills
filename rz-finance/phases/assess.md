# ASSESS Phase

> Goal: Establish a clear, honest picture of your current financial position. You cannot plan a route without knowing where you are.

#### Entry Condition

First-time setup, annual review, or major life change (job change, marriage, child, relocation, inheritance).

#### Core Workflow

1. **Net worth calculation** — List all assets and liabilities. Compute net worth.
   - Assets: cash, investments (brokerage, retirement, crypto), real estate equity, business equity
   - Liabilities: mortgages, student loans, credit card debt, other obligations
   - Use current market values, not purchase prices

2. **Cash flow analysis** — Track income and expenses for the past 3 months minimum.
   - Income: salary, side income, investment income, rental income
   - Expenses: by category (housing, food, transport, health, discretionary)
   - Savings rate: (income - expenses) / income × 100

3. **Portfolio health check** — If investments exist:
   - Current asset allocation (stocks, bonds, cash, real estate, alternatives)
   - Concentration risk — is any single position > 20% of portfolio? Is it justified by conviction?
   - Fee audit — total expense ratios across all holdings
   - Business quality check — for individual stocks, do they still pass the value investing checklist?

4. **Risk profile** — Honest assessment, not aspirational:
   - Time horizon (when do you need the money?)
   - Income stability (salaried, freelance, entrepreneurial?)
   - Dependents (who relies on your income?)
   - Emotional temperament (how did you feel during the last market crash?)
   - Knowledge level (Path A or Path B investor?)

5. **Debt inventory** — All debts with interest rates, minimums, and payoff timelines.

6. **Insurance audit** — Coverage gaps (health, life, disability, property).

#### Interaction Rules

- **Demand honesty** — "I'm comfortable with risk" is not a risk profile. "During COVID crash, I panic-sold at the bottom and regretted it" is honest self-assessment
- **Challenge lifestyle inflation** — If savings rate is below 20%, ask why before moving to PLAN
- **Flag concentration risk** — A single stock > 30% of net worth requires explicit justification through the value investing checklist

#### Exit Condition

- All accounts documented with current balances
- Cash flow data covers at least 3 months
- Risk profile honestly assessed (not aspirational)
- Portfolio health checked against value investing principles
- Clear picture of starting point for PLAN phase
